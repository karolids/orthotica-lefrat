Clinical Findings Summary
    Supporting Evidence — Cumulative Fall Risk
    Appeadu & Bordoni (2023) — StatPearls
    Fall probability approximately doubles with each additional risk factor: 8% (0 factors) → 16% (1) → 32% (2) → 64% (3) → ~78% (4+). Reinforces the multifactorial, cumulative nature of fall risk in older adults.
    Nevitt et al. (1989) — JAMA 261(18):2663–2668
    Prospective study demonstrating recurrent fall risk rises from ~10% with a single risk factor to ~70% with four. Foundational evidence for cumulative risk stratification.
    Robbins et al. (1989) — Arch Intern Med 149(7):1628–1633
    The presence of just three variables — hip weakness, impaired balance, and use of four or more medications — can increase fall risk from ~12% to near certainty. Supports aggressive intervention when multiple deficits are present.
